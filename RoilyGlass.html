There are a staggering 2,027 bridges in New York City, 10 of which have landmark status. The NYC Department of Transit oversees 793 of the NYC bridges, with the rest overseen by the MTA, the Department of Parks and Recreation, Department of Environmental Protection, or Amtrak. Twenty-one of these NYC bridges connect Manhattan to other boroughs or New Jersey. Four span the East River, and 25 are movable.

Named after Giovanni da Verrazzano, the first European to explore the Atlantic coast of North America between Florida and New Brunswick, the Verrazzano-Narrows Bridge is a glorious, double-decked behemoth of a suspension bridge, connecting Staten Island and Brooklyn.

This Neo-Gothic heartbreaker holds the mantle of being one of the oldest roadway bridges in the United States, and is easily the queen of all NYC bridges. A hybrid cable-stayed/suspension bridge, the Brooklyn Bridge connects Manhattan and Brooklyn, spanning the East River. It was the first steel-wire suspension bridge ever constructed and now boasts daily traffic of some 105,679 cars.

The Queensboro is also known as the 59th Street Bridge and, since March 2011, the Ed Koch Queensboro Bridge. This double-decked cantilever beauty spans the East River, connecting Long Island City with Midtown East by way of Roosevelt Island. The Queensboro Bridge, which was completed in 1909, cost $18 million and 50 lives. The opening ceremonies featured a two-hour fireworks display.
